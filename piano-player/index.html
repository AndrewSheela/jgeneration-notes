Song Key / Scale

Sets how sharps/flats are spelled — for chord names and for the note labels on both the keyboard and falling notes. Pick your song's actual key so, e.g., D Major spells its notes D E F# G A B C# instead of using flats.

Chord Names

Leave both as "Auto" to keep detection. Pick a root + quality to relabel every occurrence of that detected chord — handy for enharmonic fixes (e.g. D#m7 → Ebm7).

Practice Tools

Instead of a fixed scroll speed, stretches exactly one measure to fill the whole screen at a time, flowing into the next as it completes.

Turns off the glow effects and caps resolution to reduce CPU load — useful when recording with OBS or on lower-powered machines.

Markers

Use the + button next to the seek bar to drop a marker at the current position, then jump back to it any time.

Export Video

Renders the video directly — no live playback, no screen sharing, no permission dialogs. Works like MuseScore's video export: it draws every frame and renders the audio separately, then combines them into a real MP4 file. Always outputs at a fixed 1280×720 HD, regardless of your browser window size. Needs Chrome or Edge.

Screen Record (fallback)

Records exactly what's on screen in real time — useful if the offline export above isn't supported in your browser. Click Start, then choose "This Tab" when your browser asks what to share. It plays the song from the start and stops automatically at the end. Records as WebM (most reliable audio/video sync); the results panel gives you a ready-made command to convert it with ffmpeg or HandBrake if you need MP4 for editing software. Shortcut: Ctrl+V (⌘V on Mac) starts or stops recording.

Song Bank

Put your .mid files in a songs folder next to index.html, and list them in songs/manifest.json. Click a song below to load and play it.
